About me

I am assistant professor (lecturer) in computer science at Durham University.The main theme of my research is security engineering and practical cryptography. I research on physical security, web security and trustworthy machine learning; however, I am always ready to explore exciting research topics.

Previously, I was a member of Secure and Resilient Systems (SRS group, formerly known as Centre for Software Reliability, CSR) at Newcastle University where I mainly researched on designing secure protocol to guarantee protection against malicious tampering. The results of my research have been published in esteemed media outlets such as The Economist, Wall Street Journal, Guardian and BBC.

I am invited expert in Device and Sensor Group of World Wide Web Consortium (W3C). There, I collaborate with other members to set new standards for security and privacy issues of sensors connectivity in modern browsers.

Also, I am interested into hardware security and secure identification of smart devices in various Internet of Things (IoT) infrastructures.
